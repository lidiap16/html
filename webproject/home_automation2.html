history

The history of chess dates back to the 6th century in India, where it originated as a game called Chaturanga. It evolved through various cultures, reaching Persia and then the Islamic world, before making its way to Europe by the 15th century, where the modern rules began to take shape. By the end of the 19th century, chess had become standardized and widely accepted, leading to its status as one of the world's most popular games today, with millions of players globally.

list of facts

  • Chess first appeared in India about the 6th century AD and by the 10th century had spread from Asia to the Middle East and Europe.
  • Chess is a required school subject in Armenia.
  • The longest official game of chess took place in 1989 that went on for 20 hours and included 269 moves.
  • The number of possible unique chess games is much greater than the number of electrons in the universe.
  • The word “Checkmate” in chess originates from the Persian phrase “Shah Mat,” which means “the King is dead.”
